Here's why regular water tank cleaning is essential:


Water stored in water tanks is constantly exposed to contamination with various pollutants:

Biological Contamination

With viruses, bacteria, and protozoa organisms may cause these diseases: hepatitis, encephalitis, stomach and intestinal infections, amoeba, giardia, cholera, and typhoid.

Chemical Pollution

Resulting from the presence of chemicals resulting from cleaning materials, pesticides, or chemical reactions , and this pollution causes poisoning or skin diseases.

Physical pollution

Through the entry of dust, soil, bird droppings or dead birds, and calcifications.

Factors contributing to the contamination of water tanks:

Effect of High Temperature

Elevated temperatures in water tanks create an ideal breeding ground for bacteria and algae, compromising water quality and safety.

Effect of Dust

RAccumulation of dust and debris in water tanks can introduce pollutants and impurities, adversely affecting water quality.

Effect of Humidity

High humidity levels can promote mold and mildew growth in water tanks, further contaminating the water supply.

Expected Issues on your health:

Consuming or using water from unclean tanks can result in gastrointestinal issues, skin infections, respiratory problems, and other health complications.

Our Process

Assessment Visit

We start by inspecting the tank's condition and identifying any visible signs of contamination or damage.

Drainage

The tank is emptied of all water, ensuring complete removal of sediment and impurities.

Scrubbing and Disinfection

Our team thoroughly cleans the interior surfaces of the tank using specialized brushes and disinfectants to eradicate bacteria and algae.

Rinsing

The tank is rinsed multiple times to remove any residual cleaning agents and debris.

Drying

We allow the tank to dry completely to prevent mold or bacterial regrowth before refilling it with clean water.

Water Quality Testing (optional)

As a final step, we conduct water quality tests to ensure that the tank is free from contaminants and meets safety standards.
